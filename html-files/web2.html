Article heading

The heading should represent the main point of the article; it should indicate what the article is about.The heading should supplement the content of your article; it should not replace its topic sentence.


Subsection

The heading gives a brief idea of the article that is to follow, whereas the sub-heading plays a supportive role to the main idea (title).Subheadings can organize content in a more efficient way, and improve the understanding of the matter to the reader.


It works as a skeletal frame of an article. When you make a list of subheadings before you write an article, it makes it easier for you to find relevant information under those sub-headings.


Another subsection

A good sub-heading is able to describe the text that follows clearly. If the sub-headings are clear, the reader can scan through them to get the exact information they seek.
